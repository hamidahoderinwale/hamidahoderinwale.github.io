A Month in Questions

March 30, 2025

I've been using Remnote consistently for the past couple of months and I like it a lot. One of my favourite features is the "daily note." Everyday, I get a new section dedicated to the day and it will normally be populated with new words that I'd learned, links and resources that I'd come across and a to-do list. I will also put questions that come up as I went through my day and perhaps a summary of the main events. I think a good number of these bullet points could be expanded into a full blog post; instead, however, I'm going to feed the notes from the past month into ChatGPT and get a list of questions inspired by them. I hope this can be an artifact of my curiosities.

Note: I didn't edit these questions.

AI, Models, and Representations

  • What does it mean to "version" not just code, but the thought process that went into it?
  • Could Datalog help us understand how LLMs encode and organize knowledge?
  • How can we represent spatial and temporal knowledge in model embeddings more transparently?
  • What is the role of diagrammatic reasoning in mechanistic interpretability?
  • What would a declarative interface for inspecting model representations look like?

Research Practice and Knowledge Production

  • Why are survey papers so undervalued despite their high utility?
  • What would a better "testbed" look like for theoretical ideas?
  • How do you measure the value of research beyond citations—perhaps by its inspirational power?
  • Could research change more if dissemination models changed? (e.g., new patronage, collaborative distillation)
  • What models of citation make sense in the era of training data aggregators and generative AI?

Systems, Semantics, and Programming

  • How do gradual static types balance expressiveness and safety?
  • What could a type system for CRDT-based declarative visual languages enable?
  • What would an IDE for building "agentic datasets" look like?
  • How can we define the “schema” of a model’s outputs for debugging and downstream tasks?
  • Can a visual diff system make GitHub more understandable for non-programmers?

Institutions, Infrastructure, and Openness

  • Why wasn't Dwarkesh's book written using the tooling he often advocates for?
  • What institutional infrastructure supports sustained, high-quality distillation (like Distill.pub)?
  • Could LLM companies fund data libraries and citation repositories like Wikipedia?
  • What would it look like if open-source thinking extended beyond code—into research workflows, testbeds, and policy ideas?
  • Could libraries be reimagined as interfaces for analogical thinking and cross-domain discovery?

Economics and Social Thought

  • Are markets efficient if they aren’t time-aware? What’s the temporal resolution of an efficient market?
  • Is taste a kind of arbitrage?
  • What are the limits of the behavioral economics approach to systemic problems?
  • Why do people donate to research non-profits? What motivates sustained funding without a clear ROI?
  • How do information economics principles apply to generative environments?

Workflow, Expression, and Pedagogy

  • What does a “working-in-public” research workflow look like in practice?
  • How do you write for translators—people who span fields and need to “carry” your work?
  • What does teaching look like when it’s reframed as an act of explaining why something matters?
  • How do we track and version procedural knowledge over time?
  • Could tests be treated like APIs: contracts that define the interface of your reasoning?
